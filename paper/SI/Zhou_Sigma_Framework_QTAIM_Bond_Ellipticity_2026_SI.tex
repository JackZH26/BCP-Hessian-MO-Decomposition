\section{Complete Benchmark Data at HF/6-31G*}
\label{sec:SI_full}

Table~\ref{tab:SI_full} reports the per-MO decomposition for all 85
unique (compound, bond) entries computed at the HF/6-31G* level. For each
entry we report the BCP density $\rho_{\text{BCP}}$, the three Hessian
eigenvalues $\lambda_1 \le \lambda_2 \le \lambda_3$, the ellipticity
$\varepsilon = \lambda_1/\lambda_2 - 1$, the eigenvalue difference
$\delta_{\text{tot}} = \lambda_2 - \lambda_1$, and the per-MO
contributions $\delta_\sigma$ and $\delta_\pi$.

The reconstruction error
$|\delta_\sigma + \delta_\pi - \delta_{\text{tot}}|/\delta_{\text{tot}}$
is below $10^{-3}$ for all entries (verified to numerical precision in the
underlying calculations; see Section~\ref{sec:recon}).

The HF/6-31G* benchmark covers 25 chemical-bond categories: pure $\sigma$
single bonds (cylindrically symmetric and asymmetric), homonuclear and
heteronuclear $\sigma + \pi$ double bonds, triple bonds, polycyclic
aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), heteroaromatic systems (5- and 6-membered
rings), conjugated multiplenes, lone-pair-induced single bonds, vacant
$p$-orbital systems, strained rings (3- and 4-membered cycles), three-membered
heterocycles, ethers/amines/sulfides, halogenated and perfluorinated
systems, sulfur oxides, donor-acceptor (Lewis adduct) bonds, ionic bonds,
and several special cases (allene, ketene, formamide, etc.).

\section{MO Localization Independence}
\label{sec:SI_lmo}

To verify that the sign pattern is robust against the choice of MO basis,
we recomputed the per-MO decomposition using three localization schemes:
Boys, Pipek-Mezey (PM), and Intrinsic Bond Orbitals (IBO). Table~\ref{tab:SI_lmo}
reports the results at HF/6-31G* for 12 paradigmatic compounds.

In all 36 (compound, scheme) combinations the sign rule
$\delta_\sigma > 0$, $\delta_\pi \le 0$ is preserved. Numerical
magnitudes vary across localization schemes by typically 5--15\%, with
larger variations in degenerate ($\varepsilon \approx 0$) systems
where the $\sigma$/$\pi$ partition is intrinsically ambiguous.

\section{Reconstruction Error Verification}
\label{sec:recon}

The per-MO decomposition (Eq.~3 of the main text) is an algebraic
identity, so the reconstruction
$\sum_i (c_i^{(\lambda_2)} - c_i^{(\lambda_1)}) = \delta_{\text{tot}}$
is exact in exact arithmetic. Numerical implementation introduces
errors only from finite-difference Hessian evaluation
(used to compute $\partial^2 \psi_i/\partial \xi_q^2$).

We use central differences with $\eps_h = 8 \times 10^{-3}$ a.u.\
(verified to give errors $< 10^{-4}$ a.u.\ on diagonal Hessian
elements). The reconstruction error
$|\delta_\sigma + \delta_\pi - \delta_{\text{tot}}|$ is below
$10^{-3}$ a.u.\ (or 0.1\% relative) in all 102 compounds tested.

The dominant numerical error is from the central-difference scheme;
analytic Hessian evaluation (planned for future work) would reduce
this error to machine precision but is unnecessary for the qualitative
sign-pattern analysis.
